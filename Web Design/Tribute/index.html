Tribute to Mr Tan Kah Kee

Tan Kah Kee 陈嘉庚

Businessman · Leader · Philanthrophist

OVERVIEW
business_center Business
Worked and thrived in agricultural business starting small from an apprentice to one the largest titans whose enterprises created over 30 000 jobs. Known as the 'Henry Ford of Malaya', he also successfully founded a Chinese newspaper.
public Leader
Being the chairman of Chinese Chamber of Commerce as well as contributions such as being a social activist during tumultuous times, aiding relief efforts such as the Fujian and Guangdong Flood Relief Fund, his leadership experience should never be doubted.
monetization_on Philanthrophist
Donated million of dollars to fund the building of more schools all around South East Asia, hoping to provide quality education, believing education was the key to preparing the young for the demands of a modern society. Overall, he sets up more than 15 schools in Singapore and China.
21 October 1874

Born in Xiamen, Fujian province.

1890

At age 16, he comes to Singapore to work in his father's company - Chop Soon Ann Rice Company.

1891 - 1898

Took over the management of Chop Soon Ann gradually, including sago and pineapple factories.

1903

Collapse of the family business due to family embezzlement and corruption.

1904 - 1905

Sets up a pineapple cannery, Sin Lee Chuan (Xinlichuan), on a limited budget of $7,000. Buys over Jit Sin for $17,000, when its senior partner passed away, and becomes its sole owner.

1907

Sets up Tao Nan School.

1912 - 1913

Sets up Ai Tong School in Singapore and Jimei Primary School in China.

1912 - 1914

Built a business empire from rubber plantations, manufacturing, sawmills, canneries, real estate, import and export brokerage, ocean transport and rice trading. At its peak, he was well known as the 'Henry Ford of Malaya'.

1915

Sets up Chong Hock Girls’ School.

1918

Sets up Nanyang Girls’ School (which becomes Nanyang Girls’ High School in 1930) in Singapore and Jimei Normal School and Secondary School in China.

1919

Sets up Nanyang Chinese High School, the first Chinese institution of higher learning in Southeast Asia to be set up by the Chinese community. Morever, he sets up Jimei kindergarten and Xiamen University, making him the first Chinese to have founded a major modern university. Also he founds Tan Kah Kee & Co.

1920

In business, the company sets up Sumbawa Rubber Manufacturer, a manufacturing complex at Sumbawa Road producing various rubber goods. In education, he sets up Jimei Marine School and Commercial School.

1923

Sets up Chinese newspaper Nanyang Siang Pau. Becomes the chairman of Ee Ho Hean Club, an exclusive club for Chinese businessmen.

1925

Tan Kah Kee & Co is valued at an estimated $7.8 million

1929

Becomes the chairman of Singapore Hokkien Huay Kuan. Among his many reforms, Tan establishes an education department to administer Tao Nan School, Ai Tong School and Chong Fook Girls’ School, and to subsidise eight other schools established by the Hokkien community. Moreover, he donates $10,000 to Raffles College (which later becomes the University of Malaya in 1949, and University of Singapore in 1961; it is today the National University of Singapore).

1937 - 1941

Leads fund-raising efforts for the China Relief Fund in Southeast Asia and raises five billion yuan during the five-year period from the beginning of the war, in the form of remittances to families and donations. In 1941, he also sets up Nanyang Normal School while becoming the chairman of the Singapore Chinese Mobilisation Council to assist the government in the civil defence of Singapore.

1959

Establishes the Overseas Chinese Museum in Xiamen.

1960

Donates 5 million yuan to support his proposal to build an Overseas Chinese History Museum in Beijing.

12 August 1961

Passes away in Beijing and was given a state funeral by the Chinese government.

Today

Many statues are erected to acknowledge his philanthropy, hoping that such altruistic movement could continue. In Singapore, the Tan Kah Kee Scholarship Fund, which later became known as the Tan Kah Kee Foundation, as well as naming an MRT station after him was done in memory of this philanthropy.
